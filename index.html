Dementia Types 

Types of Dementia Probable Cause Symptoms
Alzheimer's Disease Unknown exact cause, involves plaques and tangles in the brain Memory loss, confusion, difficulty with language, impaired reasoning
Vascular Dementia Problems with blood supply to the brain, often following a stroke Memory loss, Problems with reasoning, planning, judgment, and thought processes
Lewy Body Dementia Abnormal protein deposits called Lewy bodies in the brain Memory loss, visual hallucinations, motor control issues
Frontotemporal Dementia Progressive loss of nerve cells in frontal and temporal lobes Memory loss, Personality changes, difficulty with language, behavioral issues
Mixed Dementia Combination of Alzheimer’s disease and vascular dementia Memory loss, Problems with reasoning
Parkinson’s Disease Dementia Progression of Parkinson's disease leading to cognitive decline Cognitive decline, mood swings, psychiatric problems
Huntington’s Disease Genetic disorder causing progressive breakdown of nerve cells Cognitive decline, mood swings, psychiatric problems
Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease Infectious proteins called prions causing rapid neurological decline Severe Memory loss, Rapid neurological decline, coordination issues
Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus Abnormal increase of cerebrospinal fluid in the brain's ventricles Memory loss, Walking difficulties, urinary incontinence, coordination issues, hallucinations
Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome Deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1), often related to chronic alcoholism Severe memory problems, coordination issues, hallucinations
